Tone Guide
Style

William Zinsser, On Writing Well. Strip every word that is not pulling weight. Use short, declarative sentences. No throat-clearing. No dramatic flourishes.

Voice

Corporate but human. Speak to a C-suite audience without losing credibility with engineers. Confident, not arrogant.

Rules

No buzzwords: “scalable,” “seamless,” “leverage,” “synergy,” “holistic,” or “robust.”

No em dashes or drama.

NO "honestly" EVER.

No “it’s not X, it’s Y” constructions.

No superlatives unless earned, such as “best,” “most,” or “highly.”

No passive voice.

No hedging, such as “helps to,” “works to,” or “aims to.”

Use active, direct verbs.

Use one idea per sentence.

Cut any word that adds no meaning.

Tone in Practice

Wrong: “I bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions.”

Right: “I make sure what engineering builds is what the business asked for.”

Wrong: “Systems are built to handle high traffic, large datasets, and growing demands.”

Right: “I build for where the business is going, not where it is.”

Audience

Primary: C-suite leaders, including CEOs, CTOs, and COOs. They care about outcomes, accountability, and return on investment.

Secondary: Technical leads who vet the claims.
